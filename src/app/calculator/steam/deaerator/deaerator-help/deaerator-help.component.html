Deaerator Calculator
Determines the required water and steam flows for a required feedwater mass flow. A deaerator is a tank used to remove dissolved gases from the feedwater before being sent to the boiler. Steam is commonly used as the heat source for the deaerator.
Input deaerator conditions and required feedwater mass flow to determine the required water and steam flows.

Measurement Method
Choose a secondary steam property (Temperature, Specific Enthalpy, Specific Entropy, or Quality) to further characterize the generated steam.
Temperature Value
The temperature of the inlet water.
Specific Enthalpy Value
Specific Enthalpy (h) is the combination of the steam's internal energy and the work needed to make room for the material, compared to the standard state, in terms of mass.
Specific Entropy Value
Specific Entropy (s) is a measure of the amount of energy which is unavailable to do work, compared to the standard state, in terms of mass.
Saturated Quality Value
Quality is the vapor mass percentage of the steam/water mixture. Quality is zero for liquid water and 1 for steam; a value between 0 and 1 indicates mixed flow (both liquid and vapor).
