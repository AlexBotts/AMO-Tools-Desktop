Establish Gas Density Help
This section establishes a gas density as a reference to establish the gas density within the fan. The location for which base gas density data is inputted could be one of the Planar Locations 1 through 5, or for an alternate location outside of Planes Nos. 1 through 5 such as in the plant general spaces or the outdoors (i.e. if the fan draws ambient air from plant spaces or the outdoors).
Gas Type
Select either ‘Air’ or ‘Other Gases’ depending on the type of gas.
Method to Establish Gas Density
There are four options for establishing gas density:
Method Description
Relative Humidity % Calculate base gas density from dry bulb temperature, static pressure, barometric pressure, gas specific gravity, and gas humidity data.
Wet Bulb Temperature Calculate base gas density from dry bulb temperature, static pressure, barometric pressure, gas specific gravity, wet bulb temperature and specific heat of the gas.
Gas Dew Point Calculate base gas density from dry bulb temperature, static pressure, barometric pressure, gas specific gravity, and gas dew point.
Use Custom Density If gas density is already known, it can be manually input.
