Compressed Air Calculators

Leak Loss Estimator - Bag Method

This calculator estimates the leakage losses in a compressed air system using the bag method.

Pneumatic Air Requirement

This calculator helps in computing the quantity of air required by a specific single acting or a double acting piston cylinder compressor. The design specs of the compressor are entered in the calculator and the quantity of air needed is generated.

Receiver Tank Sizing Calculator

This calculator uses one of four methods to calculate the required size of the receiver tank. The receiver tank is used to store a supply of compressed air and assure a steady supply without excessive line pulsations or frequent loading and unloading of the compressor. There are four methods to calculate the required size of the receiver tank.

Usable Air Capacity Calculator

This calculator computes the quantity of compressed air that is actually available for use. When air receivers are installed, the system's pressure profile and lack of storage limit the effectiveness of compressed air energy storage. Hence this calculator proves useful in such a context.

Pipe Sizing

This calculator helps in finding the pipe diameter when the volumetric flow velocity, pressure, and design velocity are known.

Velocity in the Piping

This calculator helps in finding the velocity of compressed air through all the different piping involved in the system.

System - Capacity

This calculator is used to find the total air quantity that the compressed air system can contain at any instant of time, including the air in the pipes and receivers. The total capacity of the compressed air system can be obtained by adding the volumes of pipes and receivers.

Operation Cost Calculations

This calculator helps in finding the cost of operation of the compressor in both fully and partially loaded instances.
