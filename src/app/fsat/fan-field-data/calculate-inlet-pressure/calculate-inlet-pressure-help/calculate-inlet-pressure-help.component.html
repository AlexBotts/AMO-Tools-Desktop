Estimate Inlet Pressure

This calculator is to estimate inlet pressure when inlet pressure cannot be directly measured, by estimating potential pressure losses. Losses can be estimated using information from fan/accessory manufacturers or by consulting references such as AMCA Publication 201 â€“ Fans and Systems.


Inlet Loss

Pressure loss due to the geometry of the entrance pipe.

Inlet Ductwork Loss

Pressure losses due to leaks in the ductwork or friction between the air stream and the ductwork, in cases with extremely long duct runs.

System Damper Loss

Pressure losses due to dampers in the fan system upstream of the fan.

Air Treatment Loss

Pressure loss due to inlet air treatment, such as a mist eliminator (removes the rain from incoming fresh air before entering the air handling unit). Poor treatment of incoming air leads to wet filters and an increase in pressure loss.

Flow Measurement Loss

Pressure losses due to a measuring air flow. Common flow measurement techniques can disrupt inlet air flow and reduce pressure.

Inlet Damper Loss

Pressure losses due to dampers at the inlet of the fan.

Process Requirements (Suction Side)

Pressure losses due to system components upstream of the fan.

Inlet System Effect Loss

Loss in pressure due to system effects. Duct turns at the inlet of the fan result in adverse aerodynamics, referred to as system effects (causing non-uniform loading of fan impeller, swirling flow, inability to develop a uniform flow profile on discharge). To minimize system effect loss, minimize the number of curves in the inlet ductwork.
